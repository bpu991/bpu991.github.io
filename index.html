Berklee College of Music
Student Music Therapist
Feb 2017 2021 - May 2019

During my time as a student music therapist at Berklee College of Music, I worked hands-on with five different clinical populations as part of my undergraduate practicum program. These populations ranged from children with special needs, to veterans with mental health disorders, all the way to patients with traumatic brain injuries (TBI). During these sessions, I planned and orchestrated both individual and group music therapy interventions where music was used to address non-musical objectives. For example, when working with patients diagnosed with depression, music would be used as a tool for self-expression, allowing patients to express themselves through song writing and performance. Additionally, when working with patients with TBI, rhythmic techniques would be employed to target gait.

Despite being a relatively new form of rehabilitation, the transformative impact that music has on the brain has been widely documented in research studies and I was excited to continue to advance the boundaries of this field. However, the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic forced me to re-think my career path, ultimately leading me to pivot towards learning how to code. Although I no longer practice music therapy, those years of experience showed me that I'm passionate about helping others, whether through hands-on session leadership or indirectly through the software I develop.
